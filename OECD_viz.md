## Beeswarm Plot
The plot shown below illustrates the general government debt (in the form of % of total GDP) of OECD countries over time (from 1996 to 2018). The size of the circles depicts the value of the government debt per country per year. The varying sizes of the cirles for each country show how the government debt has changed over the years. The plot also allows us to compare the relative sizes of debt between countries in a particular year.

## Comparing the Visualizations
The beeswarm plot allowed the reader to view the data without the points being overlapped. It showed how the government debt varied over the years for each country. The sizes of each circles made it easier to detect a trend over the years for different countries. However, the large number of datapoints (one per country per year) made it difficult to understand the story the graph is telling. 

As compared to te beeswarm plot, the boxplot is less busier (expect for the number which could not be deleted). It makes it easy for the reader to compare the variations in government debt over the years between countries. From the graph, it was easy to figure out how Japan, Canada and US compare to each other. A drawback of this plot as compared to the beeswarm one was that it did not illustrate the temporal data. It just shows the variation over the years (not necessarily how the variation occured).
